Open with the studio. Cut to the mud.

He was born on Christmas Eve in Kisumu and raised on a mud floor in Lugari, Kakamega, where a kerosene lamp did a streetlight's job. He was named after a grandfather who built shops in a place the village then named after him. The nickname Roylandz became a brand before the boy understood what a brand was. Twenty-four years later, two million Kenyans watch him at half past seven, and he still introduces himself the same way: my name is Eugine, formerly Roylandz.

The Kemoview Boy

There were chickens on the floor and a roof that argued with the rain, but there were also books, and a radio nobody was allowed to switch off when the news came on. The first time anyone laughed at one of his jokes in public was in a church play. He has been working an audience ever since.

The world listens louder when you speak from scars, not ego.

Campus & The Overview

At TIBS he did the thing every young presenter is supposed to do, which is too much. He hosted, he anchored, he edited the news magazine, he ran the talent show, and he built The Overview Show out of a livestream that lived on stubbornness alone. It ran weekly for two years. Discipline before fame is the only way fame ever sticks.

The Seat

When PPP TV opened the chair next to Lucy Ogunde, the channel was looking for a presenter. What it got was a strategist. He took the Head of Digital seat on the way in, co-hosts Urban News at 2PM and 7:30PM, runs Campus Xposure, co-anchors The Nairobi Podcast, and drove the channel past 700,000 subscribers. The numbers are not the point. The point is the channel built a youth strategy and the youth strategy built a channel.

Build it. Brand it. Believe it.

What the work is for

On a Monday it is for the boy on a mud floor watching the TV with the door propped open. On a Saturday it is for the schools the Urban Gang Tour rolls into, twenty teenagers with their first microphone who walk out knowing how to use one. On a Sunday he sits down to write. The voice is the same every day. Funny when it should be, honest when it costs. The next stop is wherever the mic is open.

★ The Dossier ★

Quick facts

Where is he from?
Born in Kisumu on December 24, 2001, and raised in Lugari, Kakamega County, in a homestead called Kemoview. Based in Nairobi today.
What does he actually do?
He is a broadcast journalist and TV host, an author, a public speaker and MC, and the founder of Roylandz Media. Head of Digital at PPP TV.
What languages does he work in?
English and Kiswahili at full professional level — he hosts, MCs and presents fluently in both, often switching inside the same show. Luhya (Maragoli/Bunyore) native. Sheng, native urban fluency. French, elementary.
What is the Urban Gang Tour?
Kenya's premier school and campus events company, co-founded with Lucy Ogunde. Ten battle lanes, a full production crew, and a stage that turns into national television.
Why "formerly Roylandz"?
Roylandz was the childhood nickname that became his first brand. He kept the line as a nod to where it started.
Where did he train?
Mahemas Primary, then Murgusi Secondary School (KCSE, 2016–2019), then a diploma in Journalism & Mass Communication at TIBS — Thika Institute of Business Studies (2020–2022) — where he graduated class valedictorian. Coursework: broadcast journalism, news writing, video production, media ethics, radio production. He cut his newsroom teeth as a reporter at Citizen TV before taking the seat at PPP TV.
Certifications?
Meta Certified Digital Marketing Associate · Google Digital Marketing Fundamentals · Digital Security for Journalists (Global Cyber Alliance).
Awards & recognition?
Male TikToker of the Year nominee — People's Choice Awards 2024. Class valedictorian — TIBS. Co-author of a published Global Cyber Alliance industry report.
Published work?
Born Broke, Built Loud — the memoir. And Protecting the Online Safety of Journalists in Africa — a Global Cyber Alliance report (2021), co-authored.
