When I took over as host and content director of Urban News on PPP TV, we were pulling decent numbers. Good, but not great.

Eighteen months later, we're at 2 million weekly viewers across PPP TV, NTV, and KBC TV.

Here's what actually moved the needle.

## 1. We Stopped Chasing Breaking News

Every youth news show in Kenya was trying to be CNN. Fast, reactive, breaking.

We went the opposite direction. We decided to be the show that _explains_ the news — not just reports it. What does this political decision mean for a 22-year-old in Mathare? How does this economic policy affect a campus student in Eldoret?

Context became our competitive advantage.

## 2. We Made the Presenter the Story

I'm not a neutral anchor. I have opinions. I have a perspective. I grew up broke in Western Kenya and I'm not going to pretend I don't see the world through that lens.

Audiences connect with authenticity. When I'm genuinely angry about something, they feel it. When I'm excited, they're excited.

## 3. Short-Form First, Long-Form Second

Every Urban News segment gets cut into a 60-second TikTok and a 3-minute YouTube short before the full episode drops. We build the audience on short-form, then pull them to the full show.

This single strategy doubled our digital reach in six months.

## The Lesson

Growth in media isn't about budget. It's about understanding your audience better than anyone else — and being willing to serve them in the format they actually consume.
