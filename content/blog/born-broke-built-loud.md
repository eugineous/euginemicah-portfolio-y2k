I grew up in Manyonyi Village, Kakamega County. We didn't have much. But we had stories.

My father, Joab Ob'bayi, was the kind of man who could hold a room with just his voice. My mother worked harder than anyone I've ever known. Between the two of them, they gave me something no school could teach — the belief that your story is worth telling.

## The Beginning

I didn't start in media because I had connections or money. I started because I had a phone, a YouTube account, and a burning need to say something.

The Overview Show was born in 2021 — co-founded with Elijah Maingi on a campus radio station at TIBS College. We had no budget, no audience, and no idea what we were doing. But we showed up every single day.

## What Changed Everything

Graduating as valedictorian in 2024 wasn't just an academic achievement. It was proof that the kid from Kakamega could compete at the highest level — and win.

But the real turning point was Urban News. When PPP TV gave me the platform to host Kenya's flagship youth news show, I understood for the first time what it meant to have reach. Not followers. Reach. The ability to change how a generation sees itself.

## The Lesson

If you're reading this from a small town, a broken home, or a situation that feels impossible — I want you to know something: the story you're living right now is the most powerful content you'll ever create.

Build loud. The world needs to hear you.
