I use AI every day. Adobe Podcast AI for audio cleanup. AI tools for content planning. Predictive analytics to decide which stories will land.

And yet — the most-watched segment I've ever produced was a 4-minute piece about a grandmother in Kibera who raised seven children on a vegetable cart income.

No algorithm told me to make that story. My gut did.

## The Real Threat Isn't AI

The real threat to African journalism isn't artificial intelligence. It's the continued outsourcing of our narratives to foreign media houses who parachute in, extract a story, and leave.

AI is just a tool. The question is: who's holding it?

## How I Use AI Without Losing My Voice

Here's my actual workflow:

1. **Research phase** — I use AI to scan trending topics, identify gaps in coverage, and surface data points I might miss
2. **Production phase** — Adobe Podcast AI cleans my audio in minutes instead of hours
3. **Distribution phase** — Analytics tools tell me when to post, what thumbnail works, which platform to prioritize

But the story itself? That comes from being in the room. From listening. From caring enough to get it right.

## The Opportunity

Africa has 1.4 billion people and the youngest population on earth. The stories here are infinite. The talent is here. The audiences are here.

What we need are African journalists who are fluent in both storytelling and technology. Not one or the other. Both.

That's the journalist I'm trying to become.
