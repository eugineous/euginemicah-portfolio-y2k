The Story
So Far

Not the highlight reel. The whole film. The mud, the laughter, the hunger, the hustle, the comeback.

I was born in Kisumu on Christmas Eve and raised in Lugari, Kakamega County, in a home where a kerosene lamp did the work a streetlight does for other kids. I grew up answering to a name that sounded like a prophecy and a place, Kemoview, that my grandfather literally built and the village named after him.

That noise I carried as a boy became a brand before I knew what a brand was. It became Roylandz. Then it became Eugine Micah again, this time on national TV.

"The world listens louder when you speak from scars, not ego."

Today I co-host Urban News with Lucy Ogunde, run Campus Xposure, co-host The Nairobi Podcast, and lead digital strategy at PPP TV. I work where media, marketing, and technology meet, and I'm building the case for African storytelling in the age of AI.

The Climb

From a borrowed name and a borrowed mic to a national newsroom.

2001

A birth in Kisumu

Born on Christmas Eve, wrapped in a jacket instead of a blanket, then raised in Lugari, Kakamega County, where the dust taught me to want more.

2020 – 2022

TIBS College, where the voice was found

Hosted and produced Rhumba Jouissance, The Overview Show, and TIBS Got Talent on TIBS FM and TIBS TV. Edited the news magazine. Learned the craft by doing it live.

2023

Citizen TV, the newsroom test

Reporting at Royal Media Services. Field assignments, live events, and the discipline of a real national newsroom.

2024

Valedictorian, then PPP TV

Graduated top of my class and stepped into Urban News and Campus Xposure as Head of Digital, helping grow the channel to 700K-plus subscribers.

Now

Roylandz Media & the AI era

Building formats, mentoring young creators, and championing AI for African storytelling. Same DNA as my grandfather: he built shops, I build stories.
